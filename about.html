The Practice

Design that moves things

"Not a designer who executes briefs. A designer who shapes programmes."

Justin Ukaegbu operates at the intersection of brand, product, and technology. His work has influenced investor decisions, shaped platforms used by hundreds of thousands of people, and been recognised at the highest levels of the international digital industry.

Graduating with a Distinction from the MA Graphic Design programme at the University of Hertfordshire, Justin built a practice that refuses to stay inside one discipline. Brand systems, digital product design, interaction design, educational technology, AI-assisted workflows — all in active professional use.

His work at Take Back The Mic alone reached 1.1 billion impressions across 50 countries. The interactive festival he designed for TBTM was nominated for a Webby Award in 2023. His design work for TBTM's digital card product directly contributed to a multi-million dollar Mastercard partnership.

In the UK, Justin created the brand identity, website, and all communications for Route to Zero from the ground up — a business-led membership organisation operating at government level, with serving MPs, a CBE, and an MBE on its board. His design directly shapes how the organisation engages Westminster, industry leaders, and the British public on net zero policy.

Now building Draw in the Air — a gesture-based learning platform for children that uses computer vision for embodied early literacy education. A product that is also a research platform, and a research platform that is also a viable product.
