The Context

Syntech Biofuel produces the most sustainable commercial fuel in the UK. They needed a design partner who could handle everything from daily social media to high-profile racing sponsorship materials to an educational outreach programme for primary schools. The work demanded both commercial polish and genuine creative ambition — a rare combination.

The Objective

Build a comprehensive brand communications system covering social media content, brochure design, and strategic documents. Design the racing team livery for the Syntech Racing BTRC programme, including the complete helmet design for driver Ryan Smith. Develop and deliver the Carbon Reduction Champion Pack — a school outreach programme using AI-assisted 3D character generation to engage Year 6 pupils in sustainability education.

The Approach

The social media system was built on a modular grid — consistent enough to be recognisably Syntech, flexible enough to cover everything from product announcements to sustainability messaging to race day content. The racing livery demanded precision: helmet graphics that read at speed, that photographed well from any angle, and that carried both the Syntech and sponsor brands without visual clutter. The school programme was the most inventive: students drew superhero characters by hand, Justin used an AI image processing and 3D rendering pipeline to transform those drawings into professional animated characters — live, in the classroom.

The Execution

Justin delivered: a complete social media content system with templates, grid layouts, and content guidelines; the Syntech Racing helmet design for BTRC driver Ryan Smith — featuring Union Jack elements, neon green Syntech branding, and sponsor integration; race day promotional materials and social media content for the British Truck Racing Championship; the Carbon Reduction Champion Pack educational materials for UK primary schools; a novel AI-assisted pipeline converting children's hand-drawn characters into 3D animated superheroes; live classroom demonstrations bringing the technology to life for students; brochure design, strategy documents, and corporate communications; and vehicle livery concepts for the Syntech fleet.

The Impact

The school outreach programme achieved 100% teacher and student satisfaction. Children who had never interacted with AI technology saw their own drawings transformed into animated 3D characters in real time. The programme was designed to replicate across multiple schools. The racing livery carried Syntech's brand at competitive motorsport events across the UK. The social media system gave Syntech a consistent, professional voice in a market where most competitors still communicate like industrial suppliers.
