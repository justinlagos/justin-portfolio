EasyJet

Out-of-home advertising campaign design for one of Europe's largest airlines — billboards, bus wraps, and digital displays bringing the brand to urban environments across the UK.

Client

EasyJet / EasyJet Holidays

Role

Campaign Designer

Timeline

2024

Scope

OOH Advertising, Billboard Design, Bus Wrap, Digital Display, Campaign Visuals

The Context

EasyJet is one of Europe's largest and most recognised airline brands. The brief called for out-of-home advertising that could cut through the noise of urban environments — designs that communicated the brand's energy and accessibility while maintaining the visual standards expected of a major international airline. The campaign spanned multiple formats: large-format billboards, bus stop digital displays, London bus wraps, and building-mounted signage.

The Objective

Design a cohesive out-of-home campaign that works across dramatically different formats and viewing distances. A building-mounted billboard seen from 200 metres demands a completely different approach to a bus stop display read at 2 metres. The campaign needed to unify these touchpoints under one visual system while optimising each format for its specific context.

The Approach

The campaign centred on bold, sky-focused photography paired with confident typography. The tagline 'Soar to New Heights with EasyJet' was designed to read at speed and at distance, with type hierarchy that ensured the brand name and call to action were the first and last things seen. Colour was used strategically — EasyJet's signature orange anchored every format, creating instant brand recognition even at the periphery of a viewer's attention. The bus wrap design treated the entire vehicle as a moving landscape — sky imagery wrapping around the bus's form to create an immersive effect.

The Execution

Justin designed: large-format billboard graphics for building-mounted installations; bus stop digital display advertising with integrated EasyJet Holidays messaging; London double-decker bus wrap design using full-vehicle photography treatment; the 'Soar to New Heights' campaign typography system optimised for high-speed viewing; a second campaign variant — 'Make Memories That Last a Lifetime' — for the EasyJet Holidays sub-brand; and format-specific adaptations ensuring every design worked perfectly in its intended physical context.

The Impact

The campaign brought EasyJet's brand into the physical urban environment with a consistency and visual impact that matched the airline's digital presence. Each format was designed not as a scaled version of one master design but as a purpose-built execution for its specific context — a principle that ensured the campaign looked intentional everywhere it appeared, from bus stops in central London to building signage at transport hubs.

"Not a scaled version of one master design, but a purpose-built execution for its specific context."
