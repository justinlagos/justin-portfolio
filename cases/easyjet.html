CAMPAIGN

EasyJet

Out-of-home advertising campaign for Europe's largest airlines, bringing the brand to urban environments

Client

EasyJet / EasyJet Holidays

Role

Designer

Year

2023

Scope

OOH Advertising, Billboard Design, Bus Wrap, Digital Display, Campaign Visuals

The Context

EasyJet is one of Europe's largest airlines. The brief required out-of-home advertising that could cut through urban noise while maintaining the visual standards expected of a major international airline. The campaign spanned multiple formats: large-format billboards, bus stop digital displays, London bus wraps, and building-mounted signage.

The Objective

Design a cohesive out-of-home campaign that works across dramatically different formats and viewing distances. A building-mounted billboard seen from 200 metres demands a different approach to a bus stop display read at 2 metres. The campaign needed to unify these touchpoints under one visual system while optimising each format for its specific context.

The Approach

The campaign centred on bold, sky-focused photography paired with confident typography. The tagline 'Soar to New Heights with EasyJet' was designed to read at speed and distance, with type hierarchy ensuring the brand name and call to action were the first and last things seen. EasyJet's signature orange anchored every format, creating instant brand recognition even at the periphery of a viewer's attention. The bus wrap design treated the entire vehicle as a moving landscape, with sky imagery wrapping around the bus's form to create an immersive effect.

The Execution

Large-format billboard graphics for building-mounted installations; bus stop digital display advertising with integrated EasyJet Holidays messaging; London double-decker bus wrap design using full-vehicle photography treatment; the 'Soar to New Heights' campaign typography system optimised for high-speed viewing; a second campaign variant 'Make Memories That Last a Lifetime' for the EasyJet Holidays sub-brand; and format-specific adaptations ensuring every design worked perfectly in its intended physical context.

OOH Campaign Format
Multi Format Design
UK Urban Deployment
2 Campaign Variants

The Impact

The campaign brought EasyJet's brand into the physical urban environment with consistency and visual impact matching the airline's digital presence. Each format was designed not as a scaled version of one master design but as a purpose-built execution for its specific context, ensuring the campaign looked intentional everywhere it appeared, from bus stops in central London to building signage at transport hubs.

"A purpose-built execution for its specific context."
